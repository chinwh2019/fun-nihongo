自転車の青切符: Japan's New Bicycle Fine System

June 2026 topic pick for practical daily-life Japanese. Level: beginner to lower-intermediate, with a focus on natural small talk, rainy-season bicycle rules, and polite confirmation phrases.

daily life rainy season workplace small talk

Topic and Context

From April 1, 2026, Japan expanded the traffic "blue ticket" system to bicycle riders. It applies to riders aged 16 and older, and covers common violations such as smartphone use while riding, ignoring signals, riding without lights, riding with earphones, and riding while holding an umbrella.

This is a good June topic because rainy season makes 傘差し運転 especially relevant, and it creates natural conversation at work, with neighbors, or when asking about local rules.

Grammar and Patterns

1. Shared observation: 〜ましたね / 〜ですね

最近、自転車のルールが厳しくなりましたね。
Use this when you want to make a soft, shared observation instead of sounding like you are complaining.

2. Soft hearsay: 〜みたいです / 〜みたいですね

反則金がけっこう高いみたいですね。
This is useful when you learned something from news or another person and do not want to sound overly certain.

3. "Need to be careful": 気をつけないといけません

雨の日は気をつけないといけませんね。
Natural for safety rules. Softer than directly telling someone, "Don't do that."

Useful Phrases and Contrast Sets

Too blunt

傘で自転車に乗ったらだめです。

Understandable, but it can sound like scolding in casual conversation.

More natural

雨の日に傘を差して乗るのも、気をつけないといけませんね。

Softer and more conversation-friendly.

  • Workplace small talk: 最近、自転車のルールが厳しくなりましたね。
  • Rainy-day comment: 雨の日は、傘差し運転に気をつけないといけませんね。
  • Checking a rule: これって反則金の対象になりますか。
  • Soft uncertainty: 対象になるかどうか、ちょっと分からないんですが。

Role-Play and Dialogue

Scenario: You are chatting with a coworker on a rainy morning. They mention riding a bicycle to the station.

同僚: 今日、雨ですね。駅まで自転車で行くつもりです。

Your goal: Respond naturally in Japanese. Mention that bicycle rules have gotten stricter and that riding with an umbrella needs care.

Model answer

そうですね。最近、自転車のルールが厳しくなりましたし、傘差し運転も気をつけないといけませんね。

This uses そうですね for agreement, 〜し to connect reasons, and 〜ませんね to keep the tone shared and gentle.

Cultural Notes

  • In Japanese small talk, it is often safer to frame rules as a shared situation: 気をつけないといけませんね.
  • 〜みたいですね helps you discuss news or rule changes without sounding like you are giving official advice.
  • For official or police-related rules, avoid overstating certainty unless you are reading from an official source. Use 確認したほうがよさそうです when unsure.
