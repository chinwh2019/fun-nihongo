Japanese lesson review

熱中症対策

Session date: 2026-06-07. Level: beginner to lower-intermediate. Focus: early-summer heatstroke prevention, workplace-safe small talk, and soft advice with 〜たほうがよさそう.

weather and health workplace small talk soft advice daily life in Japan

Topic and context

Japan starts talking about 熱中症対策 early in summer because humidity, sudden heat, and the rainy season can make people feel tired or unwell before full midsummer arrives.

Useful public-health phrases include エアコンをつける, 水分・塩分をこまめに補給する, and 高齢者へ声をかける. In daily conversation, the most useful skill is sounding caring without sounding bossy.

Why this matters for real conversation

Heat is a safe and common small-talk topic at work, in shops, and while commuting. Phrases like 蒸し暑いですね and 無理しないでくださいね let you show concern naturally.

Grammar and patterns

Vたほうがいい

水を飲んだほうがいいです。

You should drink water.

This is correct, but it can sound like direct advice depending on tone and relationship.

Vたほうがよさそう

こまめに水分補給したほうがよさそうですね。

Looks like we should hydrate regularly.

Softer and more natural for shared workplace small talk.

Pattern notes
  • 〜たほうがいいです: direct but useful advice.
  • 〜たほうがよさそうです: softer, based on the situation, good for coworkers.
  • 〜ですね: adds shared observation and warmth.

Useful phrases and contrast sets

Workplace-safe

  • 今日、かなり蒸し暑いですね。
    It's really humid today, isn't it?
  • こまめに水分補給したほうがよさそうですね。
    Looks like we should hydrate regularly.
  • 外回り、大丈夫ですか？無理しないでくださいね。
    Are you okay going out for client visits? Please don't push yourself.

Casual with friends

  • 今日やばい暑さだね。
    It's insanely hot today.
  • ちょっとコンビニで飲み物買ってくる。
    I'm gonna grab a drink at the convenience store.
  • 無理すると熱中症になりそう。
    If we push it, we might get heatstroke.

At a shop or station

  • 少し休める場所はありますか。
    Is there somewhere I can rest for a bit?
  • 気分が悪いです。水をいただけますか。
    I feel unwell. Could I have some water?

My answers and corrections

The available lesson note did not include a recorded learner attempt beyond the model answer, so this section preserves the key correction target from the lesson.

Understandable but a bit blunt

暑いですから、水を飲んでください。

This is grammatically understandable, but it sounds a little direct, like an instruction.

More natural

暑いので、こまめに水分とったほうがよさそうですね。

This feels softer because it describes the situation and uses a shared-observation ending.

Mistake review

  • Register mismatch: Direct commands like 飲んでください can sound too instructional with coworkers.
  • Naturalness: 水分補給する is useful and public-health sounding; 水分とる is more casual and conversational.
  • Softening: Add 〜よさそうですね when you want advice to feel based on the shared situation, not your personal judgment.

Shadowing practice

Repeat each line out loud. Aim for a gentle, caring tone.

Cultural notes

  • In Japanese workplace small talk, shared observations like 暑いですね are often safer than strong complaints.
  • Concern phrases such as 大丈夫ですか and 無理しないでくださいね sound natural when someone is working outside or commuting in extreme heat.
  • For strangers or staff, use polite, concrete requests: 少し休める場所はありますか or 水をいただけますか.
