Topic and context

日本ではW杯をどこで見るかが話題になりやすく、DAZNの「月額980円」という料金表示についても、年間契約や総額を確認したほうがよいという反応が出ています。

This is useful because real life in Japan often involves checking plans, apps, campaigns, and cancellation rules. The safest Japanese is not accusatory. It sounds calmer to say ちょっと分かりにくいですね and then ask for confirmation.

Grammar and patterns

1. 〜かどうか確認したいんですが

Use this when you want to check whether something is true. It sounds polite, soft, and practical.

途中で解約できるかどうか確認したいんですが。
ポイントが付くかどうか確認したいんですが。

2. 〜みたいですね

Use this when you are not stating something too strongly. It means “it seems like...”

年間契約みたいですね。
思ったより高くなりそうですね。

3. 〜たほうがよさそうです

A useful soft-advice pattern. It is less forceful than direct advice.

総額を確認したほうがよさそうです。

Useful phrases and contrast sets

Too blunt

この表示はだましです。

Understandable, but too accusatory for normal workplace small talk or customer support.

Natural and safer

この表示、ちょっと分かりにくいですね。

Soft criticism. Natural in workplace conversation.

Mistake review

  • Too direct: Avoid jumping straight to words like だまし or 詐欺 in ordinary conversation.
  • Missing softness: Add ちょっと, みたい, よさそう, or んですが to reduce pressure.
  • Total cost check: For online plans, ask about 総額, not only 月額.

Cultural notes

  • In Japanese workplace small talk, soft agreement first is often smoother: そうですね。でも...
  • For customer support, use confirmation language rather than accusation: 確認したいんですが.
  • When a price display looks cheap, Japanese users often check whether it is monthly, annual, campaign-only, or cancellable.
