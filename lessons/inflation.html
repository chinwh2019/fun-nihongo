Japanese lesson review

スーパーでの値上げラッシュ

Session date: 2026-06-07 JST. Focus: talking naturally about grocery price increases in Japan, especially rice and everyday supermarket items.

shopping small talk natural phrasing beginner to lower-intermediate

Topic and context

In early June 2026, Japanese sources were discussing another wave of food price increases. 帝国データバンク reported that many飲食料品 were scheduled for price hikes, and everyday items such as seasonings, natto, canned food, instant noodles, mayonnaise, seaweed, and coffee were part of the wider conversation.

This topic is useful because people in Japan often talk about prices in casual, softened ways. Instead of bluntly saying something is expensive, it is common to make a shared observation:

最近、いろいろ高くなりましたね。

"Lately, lots of things have gotten expensive, haven't they?"

Grammar and patterns

1. 前より + adjective / verb

  • 前より高いです。
    It is more expensive than before.
  • 前より量が少ない気がします。
    I feel like there is less than before.
  • 前より買い物にお金がかかります。
    Shopping costs more than before.

2. 気がします for softening

高いです is clear, but 高い気がします sounds softer and more conversational. It leaves room for the other person to agree.

3. Spaced review: 〜たほうがよさそう

From the heatstroke lesson, reuse this softer advice pattern:

最近いろいろ高いので、セールのものを買ったほうがよさそうですね。

Since lots of things are expensive lately, it seems better to buy things on sale.

Useful phrases and contrast sets

Polite / safe

  • 最近、食料品がけっこう高くなりましたね。
  • 前より高い気がします。
  • これはセールになっていますか。
  • 少し安いものはありますか。

Casual

  • また値上げかあ。
  • 前より高くない？
  • 最近、買い物するだけでけっこうかかるね。
Naturalness contrast

A bit blunt:

これは高いです。

More natural:

これ、前より高くなりましたね。

これ、前よりちょっと高くなった気がしますね。

Role-play and dialogue

Scenario: You are at a supermarket with a coworker after work.

同僚: 納豆もカップ麺も、前より高くなりましたね。

Your goal: Say, "Yeah, I feel like groceries have gotten expensive lately. We should probably buy things on sale."

Suggested answer

そうですね。最近、食料品が高くなった気がします。セールのものを買ったほうがよさそうですね。

同僚: 本当ですね。前はもう少し安かった気がします。

Next practice prompt: Reply: "Yeah. I think I'll buy it when it's on sale."

One possible reply

そうですね。セールのときに買おうと思います。

My answers and corrections

Learner answer:

そうですね、米も高くなりました

Corrected natural version:

そうですね。お米も高くなりましたよね。

Even more natural:

そうですね。最近、お米もかなり高くなりましたよね。

Why: 米 is understandable, but お米 sounds more natural in everyday conversation. Adding よね makes it feel like shared small talk.

Mistake review

Pattern to watch: understandable but slightly plain or abrupt wording.

米も高くなりました。

Upgrade: use natural noun choice + shared-feeling ending.

お米も高くなりましたよね。

Cultural notes

  • Price complaints in Japanese small talk are often softened with よね, 気がします, or けっこう.
  • At a shop, avoid sounding like you are blaming the staff. Ask neutrally: これはセールになっていますか。
  • With coworkers, shared observations are safer than strong complaints: 最近、食料品がけっこう高くなりましたね。
